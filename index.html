Frequently Asked Questions

What is Bitcoin sovereignty and why is it important for estate planning?

Bitcoin sovereignty refers to maintaining complete control over Bitcoin holdings without reliance on third parties. In estate planning, it ensures families can preserve and transfer wealth across generations while maintaining privacy, security, and control. The KEEP Protocol provides a comprehensive framework for implementing Bitcoin sovereignty in professional estate planning.

How does Executive Order 6102 relate to modern Bitcoin sovereignty?

Executive Order 6102 (1933) required Americans to surrender gold to the government. This historical precedent demonstrates the importance of monetary sovereignty. Bitcoin provides protection against similar future actions through decentralized, censorship-resistant technology. Firm6102 helps families maintain sovereign control of their Bitcoin wealth.

What are the key components of a Bitcoin sovereignty estate plan?

A comprehensive Bitcoin sovereignty estate plan includes: (1) Secure self-custody implementation with multi-signature security, (2) Legal protection through Bitcoin dynasty trusts and proper documentation, (3) Heir education and access procedures, and (4) Future planning for technological and regulatory changes.

How do government Bitcoin strategic reserves impact family estate planning?

Government Bitcoin strategic reserves create both opportunities and considerations for family estate planning. Positive impacts include increased Bitcoin legitimacy and potential price appreciation. Planning considerations include regulatory changes, tax implications, and the importance of maintaining sovereignty principles.
